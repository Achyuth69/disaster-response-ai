HYDERABAD FLOOD ZONE AND RISK ASSESSMENT DATA
==============================================

FLOOD RISK CLASSIFICATION
--------------------------
Zone A (EXTREME RISK): Inundation within 2 hours of heavy rainfall (>100mm/hr)
Zone B (HIGH RISK): Inundation within 6 hours of sustained rainfall (>50mm/hr)
Zone C (MEDIUM RISK): Inundation possible during prolonged rainfall (>30mm/hr for 12+ hours)
Zone D (LOW RISK): Flooding unlikely except in extreme events (>200mm/24hr)

ZONE A — EXTREME FLOOD RISK AREAS
------------------------------------
1. Dilsukhnagar (Moosarambagh area)
   - Lowest elevation in city: 478–485m
   - Population: 180,000
   - Historical floods: 2000, 2005, 2009, 2020 (worst: 2020, 3m inundation)
   - Drainage capacity: 15mm/hr (severely inadequate)
   - Evacuation time required: 90 minutes minimum

2. Amberpet / Tarnaka Low-lying Areas
   - Elevation: 488–495m
   - Population: 120,000
   - Musi River overflow risk: HIGH when gauge > 6m
   - Key vulnerable sites: Amberpet slums, Tarnaka railway colony

3. Malkajgiri Nala Zones
   - Elevation: 495–505m
   - Population: 95,000
   - Nala (drainage channel) overflow risk: HIGH
   - Blocked drainage points: 12 identified

4. Mehdipatnam / Tolichowki
   - Elevation: 485–500m
   - Population: 200,000
   - Mir Alam Tank overflow risk: MEDIUM-HIGH
   - Underpass flooding: Confirmed at 30mm/hr rainfall

5. LB Nagar / Saroornagar
   - Elevation: 480–498m
   - Population: 250,000
   - Saroornagar Lake overflow risk: HIGH
   - Last major flood: 2020 (2.5m inundation, 15 deaths)

ZONE B — HIGH FLOOD RISK AREAS
--------------------------------
6. Khairatabad / Masab Tank
   - Elevation: 490–510m
   - Population: 150,000
   - Hussain Sagar overflow risk: MEDIUM
   - Masab Tank (historic water body, now urban): Overflow at 50mm/hr

7. Uppal / Boduppal
   - Elevation: 490–510m
   - Population: 180,000
   - Industrial drainage overflow risk: HIGH
   - Nala blockages: 8 identified

8. Secunderabad Cantonment Low Areas
   - Elevation: 505–515m
   - Population: 80,000
   - Hussain Sagar overflow risk: LOW-MEDIUM

ZONE C — MEDIUM FLOOD RISK AREAS
----------------------------------
9. Kukatpally / KPHB
   - Elevation: 500–520m
   - Population: 300,000
   - Recommended as staging/relief area

10. Malkajgiri Elevated Areas
    - Elevation: 510–525m
    - Population: 60,000

ZONE D — LOW FLOOD RISK AREAS (SAFE ZONES / RELIEF CAMPS)
-----------------------------------------------------------
11. Banjara Hills
    - Elevation: 580–620m
    - Recommended: Relief camp location

12. Jubilee Hills
    - Elevation: 570–600m
    - Recommended: Medical staging area

13. Begumpet
    - Elevation: 540–560m
    - Recommended: Command center, helipad operations

MUSI RIVER FLOOD STAGES
------------------------
Normal: 0–3m gauge level
Watch: 3–5m gauge level (monitor closely)
Warning: 5–6.5m gauge level (prepare evacuation)
Danger: 6.5–8m gauge level (mandatory evacuation of Zone A)
Extreme Danger: >8m gauge level (Zone A + B evacuation, bridge closures)

HISTORICAL FLOOD DATA
----------------------
2000 Flood: 3m inundation in Dilsukhnagar, 50 deaths, 100,000 displaced
2005 Flood: 2m inundation, 30 deaths, 75,000 displaced
2009 Flood: 1.5m inundation, 15 deaths, 40,000 displaced
2020 Flood (worst in 100 years): 3.5m inundation in Zone A, 50 deaths, 200,000 displaced
  - Trigger: 190mm rainfall in 24 hours + Osman Sagar release
  - Response time: 6 hours to full deployment (too slow)
  - Lesson: Pre-position boats in Zone A before monsoon peak

DRAINAGE INFRASTRUCTURE
------------------------
Total storm drains: 1,847 km
Functional capacity: 35mm/hr (design standard)
Current effective capacity: 22mm/hr (due to encroachment and silting)
Pumping stations: 47 (12 non-functional as of last audit)
Critical pump stations: Dilsukhnagar (2 pumps), Mehdipatnam (3 pumps), LB Nagar (2 pumps)
